Rogue Nebula

Summary

A project made for the Video Game Design Club during their summer session. Endlessly plow through waves of enemies in the persuite of a high score. A game that is heavily inspired by classic arcade games like Galaga, Defender 1981, and Space Invaders. This game features a scrolling 3D envirnment, a reactive user interface, vibrant special effects, and an assortment of enemy behaviors.

Gameplay

The player first must choose a name to go by in order to get a leaderboard entry, if they do not choose a name they are "anonymous". The player is then imediatly thrusted into the first wave of enemies, in order to survive the player must dodge their flurry of bullets and fire back. The player is not completely helpless through as they have abilities and a secondary weapon to help them. Every eight waves the game starts upping the difficulty, after wave sixteen you are on the hardest difficulty and from there on you must try to survive an endless horde. This game is not easy but a verteran bullet hell player might get high into the score system.

Implemented Features

Name Description
High Score System The high score is persistant throughout the playthough utalizing Unity's Scriptable Object System.
Plyer Abilities The player is given a small set of unique abilites. An Evade that gives the player invunerability frames and extra speed and a Bubble Shield that also make the player invunerable for a more extended period of time. Each ability has a cooldown timer that communicates with the player's HUD to let the player know when they can use it again.
Assortment of Unique Enemies The game features four unique enemy types. A standard enemy that flys back and fourth across the screen and shoots bullets at a semi random rate, a radial enemy that shoots bullets in eight different directions, a bomber enemy that periodically drops timed bombs, and a homing enemy that tracks the player's position and shoots bullets at the players direction.
Dynamic Wave System The game reacts to the player's wave count. Every ten waves the game will intraduce new enemies and make harder varients of basic enemies to up the challenge.
Particle System and Shaders The game features unique hit and destroy particles heavily inspired by arcade games. The explosion particles not only use high quality explosions from Unity's particle pack but also voxels to elicite a 3D arcade-like effect. The hit effects reflect the type of entity damaged and change color using a combination of lightning particles and 2D sprites. The player is given a special shader fresnel effect when hit.
Music and Sound Music and Sound made by a very talented member of the club, Edrick Fajardo. (efajard4@asu.edu)
